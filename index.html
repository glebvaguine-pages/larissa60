ü•ê Baked Brie en Cro√ªte

Timing: 4:00 PM - 4:30 PM | Serves: 4-5 | Temp: 425¬∞F

Ingredients:

  • 1 sheet puff pastry (thawed, kept cold)
  • 1 wheel brie cheese (8-10 oz)
  • 3-4 tbsp honey
  • 1/4 cup dried cranberries
  • 1/4 cup candied pecans, chopped
  • 1 egg, beaten (for egg wash)

Instructions:

  1. Preheat oven to 425¬∞F. Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. Prep pastry: Unfold cold puff pastry on a lightly floured surface. Roll gently to smooth any creases.
  3. Layer ingredients: In the center of the pastry, drizzle 2-3 tbsp honey. Sprinkle dried cranberries and chopped candied pecans over the honey.
  4. Add brie: Place the brie wheel directly on top of the honey-nut mixture.
  5. Wrap: Gather the four corners of the pastry and bring them up over the brie, pleating and pressing to seal at the top. Trim any excess pastry.
  6. Flip: Carefully flip the wrapped brie so the seam side is down on the baking sheet.
  7. Egg wash: Brush the entire pastry surface generously with beaten egg for a golden finish.
  8. Bake: Bake for 15-20 minutes until the pastry is deep golden brown and puffed. Watch carefully‚Äîdon't overbake or the brie will harden inside.
  9. Rest: Let cool for 5 minutes before transferring to the charcuterie board.
  10. Serve: Drizzle with additional honey and serve warm with crackers and bread.

ü¶Ü Duck √† l'Orange with Bigarade Sauce

Timing: 4:30 PM - 5:30 PM | Serves: 4-5 | Target temp: 135-140¬∞F internal

Duck Ingredients:

  • 3 large boneless duck breasts (6-8 oz each), skin-on
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Bigarade Sauce Ingredients:

  • 3 tbsp granulated sugar
  • 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
  • 2-2.5 cups duck or veal stock
  • 1.25 cups fresh orange juice (4-5 oranges)
  • 1/3 cup fresh lemon juice (2-3 lemons)
  • 1-2 tbsp Grand Marnier or Cointreau
  • 2-3 tbsp cold unsalted butter (for finishing)
  • Fresh orange segments and thyme sprigs (garnish)

Instructions:

  1. Prep duck: Pat duck breasts completely dry. Score skin in a crisscross pattern, cutting 1/3-3/4 through fat layer but NOT into meat. Season both sides generously with salt and pepper. Let sit at room temperature for 20-30 minutes.
  2. Make gastrique: In a small saucepan, combine sugar and red wine vinegar. Boil over medium-high heat WITHOUT stirring until dark mahogany colored (3-4 minutes). Watch carefully!
  3. Add stock: Carefully whisk in duck or veal stock. The caramel may seize but will dissolve as you whisk. Simmer for 10 minutes.
  4. Add citrus: Add fresh orange juice, lemon juice, and Grand Marnier. Simmer until reduced by 1/3 (about 5 more minutes). Set aside and keep warm.
  5. Cold pan start: Place duck breasts SKIN-SIDE DOWN in a COLD skillet with no oil or butter. Turn heat to medium-low.
  6. Render fat: Cook for 10-12 minutes, pouring off excess fat occasionally. The skin should become deep golden brown and crispy.
  7. Flip and sear: Flip duck breasts. Increase heat to medium-high and sear meat side for 1-2 minutes.
  8. Oven finish: Transfer to oven-safe dish, skin-side up. Insert thermometer. Roast at 375¬∞F for 5-10 minutes until internal temp reaches 135-140¬∞F.
  9. REST (critical!): Remove from oven. Place on cutting board and tent with foil. Rest AT LEAST 5-10 minutes.
  10. Finish sauce: Gently reheat sauce. Remove from heat and whisk in 2-3 tbsp cold butter for sheen.
  11. Slice and serve: Slice duck on a bias. Arrange on plates. Ladle sauce around or over duck. Garnish with orange segments and thyme. Serve with pommes pur√©e and carrots.
üîë Critical Techniques: (1) Cold pan start for crispy skin, (2) Slow fat rendering (10-12 min), (3) Temperature precision 135-140¬∞F, (4) MUST rest 5-10 minutes!

ü•î Pommes Pur√©e

Timing: 4:30 PM - 5:00 PM | Serves: 4-5

Ingredients:

  • 2.5-3 lbs small waxy potatoes (Yukon Gold or Ratte), whole and unpeeled
  • 1.5 lbs (6 sticks) unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1.25-1.5 cups heavy cream or whole milk, heated
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Boil potatoes: Place whole, unpeeled potatoes in a large pot. Cover with cold salted water. Bring to boil, then reduce to simmer. Cook 15-18 minutes until fork-tender.
  2. Drain and peel: Drain thoroughly. While still very hot, carefully peel the potatoes.
  3. Pass through sieve: Immediately pass hot potatoes through a fine-mesh sieve or food mill into a warm bowl. Never use a food processor or potato masher!
  4. Add butter: Place bowl over very low heat. Add cold cubed butter bit by bit, whisking vigorously after each addition. The cold butter will emulsify into the hot potatoes.
  5. Add cream: Slowly drizzle in hot milk or cream while whisking constantly. Consistency should be loose and barely hold its shape‚Äîlike soft-serve ice cream.
  6. Season: Add salt generously to taste. Pommes pur√©e needs more salt than you think!
  7. Keep warm: Cover and keep over very low heat until plating. Whisk before serving to restore silkiness.
üîë Key Technique: Pass through sieve (not mash), add COLD butter to HOT potatoes for emulsification, use generous butter‚Äîabout 1 lb butter per 2 lbs potatoes!

ü•ï Honey Glazed Carrots

Timing: 4:45 PM - 5:00 PM | Serves: 4-5

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs carrots, peeled and sliced on the bias
  • 1/2-3/4 cup chicken broth
  • 2-3 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • Fresh lemon juice (from 1/2 lemon)
  • 2 tsp fresh thyme, minced
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Combine: In a large skillet, combine sliced carrots, chicken broth, and honey.
  2. Simmer covered: Bring to simmer over medium heat. Cover and cook 5-7 minutes until tender-crisp.
  3. Reduce to glaze: Remove lid. Increase heat to medium-high. Let liquid reduce and thicken into a syrupy glaze, stirring occasionally (5-6 minutes).
  4. Finish with butter: When glaze coats carrots and liquid is mostly evaporated, add cold butter. Stir gently until butter melts and carrots are glossy.
  5. Season: Remove from heat. Add squeeze of fresh lemon juice and sprinkle with minced thyme. Taste and adjust‚Äîmore honey if sweeter, more lemon if tangier. Season with salt and pepper.
  6. Keep warm: Keep warm over very low heat or covered until plating.
